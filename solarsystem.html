The Solar System


Earth

Earth is the third planet in the solar system, is 150 million KM away from our sun and is considered to be in the goldilocks zone, the position a planet can be around a star that allows life to thrive.

Our little blue rock is over 4.5 billion years old and has sustained life for more then half that time. We are merely a speck on it's impossible to imagine timescale.


Moon

The Moon is Earth's celestial satellite that orbits our planet ever 28 days. The Moon is tidal locked meaning the same side always faces the planet. This is due to it's axis rotation and orbit around the Earth matching.

It's said that the Moon formed when two Mars sized planets smashed into each other. The end result was Earth and the Moon.


Mercury

Mercury is the first planet in the solar system and is 58 million KM away from the sun. Being so close, it's impossible for life to form here as temperatures range from 430 degrees Celsius during the day and minus 180 degrees Celsius during the night.

A year on Mercury is only 88 earth days with it's short orbit around the sun.


Venus

Venus is the second plaent in the solar system and much like Mercury, is too close to the Sun to sustain life. Dispite it's 108 million KM distance away from the Sun, Venus is actually hotter than Merury.

A day on Venus lasts longer than a year, spins clockwise on it's axis and has 90 times the atmospheric pressure of Earth


Mars

Mars is the fouth planet in our solar system and is considered to be a planet that once harboured life and could possibly do so again with terriforming efforts.

With plans to colonize Mars one day, we could soon call it humanities second home.

Mars is named after the Roman God of War and is the second smallest planet after Mercury
