The Solar System


Our Sun

The Sun is G-type star, or yellow dwarf as they're more commonly known as. It was formed from the collection of a huge nitrogen gas cloud that collasped in on itself, eventually triggering fusion and lighting up.

The Sun makes up 99.8% of all mass in the solar system, has an inner core temperature of 15 million degrees Celsius and is almost 1.4 billion KM wide.


Earth

Earth is the third planet in the solar system, is 150 million KM away from our sun and is considered to be in the goldilocks zone, the position a planet can be around a star that allows life to thrive.

Our little blue rock is over 4.5 billion years old and has sustained life for more then half that time. We are merely a speck on it's impossible to imagine timescale.


Moon

The Moon is Earth's celestial satellite that orbits our planet ever 28 days. The Moon is tidal locked meaning the same side always faces the planet. This is due to it's axis rotation and orbit around the Earth matching.

It's said that the Moon formed when two Mars sized planets smashed into each other. The end result was Earth and the Moon.


Mercury

Mercury is the first planet in the solar system and is 58 million KM away from the sun. Being so close, it's impossible for life to form here as temperatures range from 430 degrees Celsius during the day and minus 180 degrees Celsius during the night.

A year on Mercury is only 88 earth days with it's short orbit around the sun.


Venus

Venus is the second plaent in the solar system and much like Mercury, is too close to the Sun to sustain life. Dispite it's 108 million KM distance away from the Sun, Venus is actually hotter than Merury.

A day on Venus lasts longer than a year, spins clockwise on it's axis and has 90 times the atmospheric pressure of Earth


Mars

Mars is the fouth planet in our solar system and is considered to be a planet that once harboured life and could possibly do so again with terriforming efforts.

With plans to colonize Mars one day, we could soon call it humanities second home.

Mars is named after the Roman God of War and is the second smallest planet after Mercury


Jupiter

Jupiper is the fifth planet in the solar system and is the largest of four gas giants as well as the rest of the planets. Jupiter is considered a failed star as it's compositiion is that of our Sun being mostly hydrogen and helium.

Jupiter has twice the mass of rest of the planets combined, has a circumference of439,264 km and has 312 times the surface area of Earth - 61.5 billion square KM


Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet in the solar system and is definitely the most unique with its orbiting ring. Saturn's rings are thought to have evolved from the debris of two icy moons that collided and shattered a few hundred million years ago.

Saturn is the king of the moons having 146, Is a gas giant and takes 29 years to orbit the Sun


Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet in the solar system, also being a gas giant. Uranus has a unique color due to its methane gas athmosphere.

Uranus in the coldest plant in the solar system even tho it isn't the furthest away, orbits the sun on it's side and has rings that arent visable to traditional telescopes.


Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and is considered the final planet in the solar system and it too has rings that arent visible.

Compared to Earth, a year on Neptune lasts 165 years, is 30 times further away and is twice as dense as the other gas giants. It has only completed 1 orbit around the Sun since it was first discovered.
